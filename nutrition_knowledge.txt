General Nutrition Knowledge

Calories represent the total energy provided by food. Foods high in fat and sugar generally contain more calories.

Carbohydrates are the body's primary energy source. Rice, roti, dosa, and idli are common carbohydrate-rich foods in Indian diets.

Protein is essential for muscle growth, tissue repair, and enzyme production. Good protein sources include chicken, lentils (dal), paneer, chickpeas, and beans.

Fats provide long term energy and support cell growth, but excessive intake may lead to weight gain. Healthy fats include nuts, seeds, and some oils.

Dietary fiber improves digestion and supports gut health. Foods rich in fiber include vegetables, legumes, and whole grains.

Sodium regulates fluid balance but excessive sodium can increase blood pressure.

Calcium is important for bone health and muscle function.

Iron is required for red blood cell production and oxygen transport in the body.

Vitamin C helps immune function and improves iron absorption.

Folate supports DNA synthesis and is essential for cell growth.


Indian Food Nutrition Insights

Many Indian lentil dishes such as dal contain high protein and moderate carbohydrates.

Chicken based dishes tend to have high protein content.

Paneer based foods provide both protein and fats.

Vegetable based dishes often contain higher fiber and micronutrients.

Drinks such as tea, coffee, and infused waters contain very low calories.

Fermented foods such as idli and dosa provide carbohydrates with moderate protein.

Street foods such as samosa, pakora, and fried snacks contain higher fats and calories.

Soups and broths typically contain low calories but may provide minerals such as sodium and calcium.


Dietary Patterns

Balanced meals typically contain carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats.

For weight loss diets, lower calorie foods with higher fiber and protein are recommended.

For muscle gain diets, foods high in protein such as chicken, lentils, eggs, and paneer are beneficial.

Hydration and micronutrients from fruits and vegetables support overall health.


Nutrient Roles

Protein supports muscle repair and immune function.

Carbohydrates provide quick energy.

Fats support hormone production and long term energy storage.

Fiber supports digestion and gut microbiome health.

Calcium supports bone density.

Iron prevents anemia.

Vitamin C supports immune health and collagen production.

Folate supports cell growth and DNA synthesis.
